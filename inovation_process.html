Ferro Fiddles with Cubics

Scipione del Ferro, a professor at the university of bologna was the first to come up with the solution to the cubic equation x3+ax2+bx+c=0 however, there were many unsolvable cases where the number of roots wasn’t equal to 3 because he came across the square root of negative numbers.

Cardano Cracks the Code

Girolamo Cardano however, found that if you exclude the two (what we call imaginary roots) you could find the real root. He provided a method for solving quadratic and cubic equations in his ars magna in the section on postulating a negative he calls the answer a new number terming it the “true negative”

In sixteenth century Italy, there was a major breakthrough in mathemat ics. Several Italian mathematicians figured out how to solve cubic equations and fourth degree equations as well. But as we see in Girolamo Cardano's Ars Magna (Cardano, 1968), the basic principle here was the same as in the solution of quadratic equations.

Bombelli Accnoledges Complex Numbers

Rafeal Bombelli provided methods to perform operations on complex numbers. In his Algebra, he attempted to prove imaginary numbers using constructions in two and three dimensions believing that there must be a geometric proof for his newfound class of numbers. Although unsuccessful, he provided many ideas and a basis for other mathematicians to improve upon.
