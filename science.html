Scientific Implications

Fluid Dynamics on the Complex Plane

The application of complex numbers doesn’t just stop at the theoretical world of math but it applies to many fields in science as well. Fluid Dynamics is one field where complex numbers come in handy. The navier-stokes equations model the flow of fluids with all types of boundary conditions and fluid variables. Claude-Louis Navier first invented the equations to model fluids, but Sir George Stokes popularized the use of replacing the real vector component with an imaginary vector component. This was a breakthrough in the field and would give many tricks for physicists to use for solving fluid flow.

Quantem Mechanics gets Even More Complex

Quantum Mechanics is also another field that uses complex numbers, in the case of the Schrödinger equation (a building block of modern day quantum mechanics) the complex plane is necessary in deriving the equation. Before the generalized quantum model was created by Erwin Schrödinger many before him used complex numbers to describe light particles (Einstein popularized these methods in special relativity). Schrödinger was the first to break out of classical electromagnetics with derivation of the equation.

Fourier Transforms Reality

Another extremely important concept is the fourier transform created by Jean-Baptiste Joseph Fourier, a French mathematician. The fourier transform equations utilize complex numbers and Euler’s formula. They are some of the most useful equations as they are used in many different types of science and math. They are used in fourier transform spectroscopy, quantum mechanics, signal processing, analyzing differential equations, electromagnetism and in many other sciences and math. Electromagnetism plays a huge role in electrical engineering which has greatly progressed technology and by proxy, all of humanity.
